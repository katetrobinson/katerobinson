Cincinnati Animal Clinic

Veterinary Clinic Responsive Website

01/ Project Overview

The goal of this project was to create a comprehensive, easy, and accessible responsive website for a local veterinary clinic to manage their practice.


The Cincinnati Animal Clinic needed to establish their online presence to better communicate their brand and handle all their scheduling and registration needs.

Duration

July-August 2022

Role

Product Designer
product strategy, user research, wireframing, visual design, interaction, prototyping, usability testing

02/ Understanding the Problem

Foundational research consisted of conducting user interviews and competitive audits to test early concepts, better understand what problems actually exist in the space, and thereby create a useful value proposition.

The Problem

This small business needed to free up phone lines and have an efficient system for their client management (who are also busy people) as well as registration and scheduling.

Industry Standards

Based on competitive audits, it became clear that audience and easy branding is key when the brand is reaching their audience of pets and people. Our audience is busy and urban, aspects like accessibility, modern style, and quick usability were primary needs.

User Research

User interviews were conducted with a diverse sample of users to ultimately form empathy maps and affinity diagrams, which then allowed the team to deduce user pain points. Eclectic personas with overarching user stories and journey maps were created, cultivating a higher understanding of and empathize towards primary users of the app and clients affected by the app.


All of the above, in combination with four competitive audits, determine the optimal value propositions to differentiate our veterinarian responsive online website.


Pain Points

  1. Time. Pet parents and vet staff are busy managing work, family, education, and more.
  2. Accessibility. Veterinary platforms must be equipped with assistive technologies for pet parents with varying abilities.
  3. Efficiency. Calling in and emailing are not efficient ways to register and schedule, especially as a business scales.


Sitemap

To make the app as efficient as possible before starting the design, a quick sitemap was thoughtfully constructed to best lay out all the essential needs of a veterinary clinic and their clients.

03/ Starting the design

The design process followed an iterative, lean, and responsive design approach.

Key strategy & focus areas

From busy professionals with big furry families to hard-working college students with fluffy friends, all desire an efficient online portal for quick scheduling and registration for their pets. The following allowed us to meet all needs of not only our users but the clinic's branding itself:

  1. 1. An in-depth about us page
  2. 2. A list of all provided services for newcomers
  3. 3. A built-in scheduling resource
  4. 4. A location for registering pets and filling out necessary forms
  5. 5. An emergency contact page

Wireframing

Paper wireframes were drafted first through crazy eights and how might we methodologies, paired with additional brainstorming and storyboarding before becoming digitized.

Based on user goals and competitive audits, data-driven and holistic insights and tools as well as on-the-go features and accessible tools were included in the digital wireframes, which were created to hone in on the visual structure.

04/ Refining the design

To transform from wireframe to lo-fi then hi-fi mockups, multiple usability studies were conducted once the initial designs were strategized and fully formed.

Usability studies

Usability studies were conducted after all wireframes and interactions were drafted, then after lo-fi mockups were created to create the final hi-fi design.


Through studies, it was found that the light and fun branding of the design. However, it was also found that features such as more connectivity between pages, logos, and images were needed as well as a cleaner form and calendar setup. Users would also like a secure way to make payments online.

Final Mockups

Hi-fi mockups were finalized once all data, themes, and insights were implemented.


See the final prototype here (for desktop) and here (for mobile).

Accessibility Considerations

The UserWay widget was integrated into the final designs to account for all necessary, up-to-date assistive-tech (i.e. screen readers compatibility; high contrast, dark mode, color blind friendly, and additional color modifications; text magnification; and compatibility with various language options).
