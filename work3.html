Reverie Hotels

Hotel Responsive Website

01/ Project Overview

As a part of the UX Design Institute Professional Diploma, students were tasked to design a new website or mobile app for our client. We focused specifically on the hotel booking process: how users search for and book hotel rooms online.


The end goal was to design and build a clickable prototype that can be tested with users, along with a detailed set of wireframes.

Duration

Aug-Sept 2022

Role

Product Designer
product strategy, user research, wireframing, visual design, interaction, prototyping, usability testing

02/ Understanding the Problem

Foundational research consisted of conducting user interviews, online surveying, and competitive audits to test early concepts, better understand what problems actually exist in the space, and thereby create a useful value proposition.

The Problem

The client is a new hotel. They're looking to create an online booking experience that is simple, accessible and based on a deep understanding of their target users.

Industry Standards

Based on competitive audits, it was deduced that sites tended to use an easy and simple structure booking process, but also one inclusive of all items (reviews, prices, amenities, map for location, etc.) needed to make a decision for booking. Language should be noncommittal and exploratory. Branding sets the tone of their stay for users.

User Research

User interviews through competitor usability tests were conducted with several users with high travel backgrounds to ultimately form several analyses, including affinity diagrams and user journeys. These analysis techniques allowed the team to deduce user motivations, needs, and pain points during the booking process.


In combination with five competitive audits (of individual hotels, hotel chains, and hotel comparison sites) the above analyses helped understand industry standards for hotel booking and where we can innovate to hit on users' desires.

03/ Starting the design

The design process followed an iterative and responsive design approach.

Key strategy & focus areas

Making users go from day-dreamers to day-doers, the strategy for Reverie Hotels became creating a new, imaginative experience while maintaining all the sleek features of regular hotel booking sites, including:

  1. 1. Inspired, holistic images and branding
  2. 2. Vision board capability to track day-doers' desired locations
  3. 3. Fluid site navigation and interactions
  4. 4. Extensive features such as maps, calendars, reviews, amenities and add-ons, and filter/search capabilities.

Wireframing

Paper wireframes were rapidly prototyped through crazy eights before becoming digitized.

Based on user goals and competitive audits, our strategy became clear and digital wireframes were decisioned and mocked up.

04/ Refining the design

To transform from wireframe to lo-fi then hi-fi mockups, our previous user research and consequent analyses of the work were analyzed in order to integrate all necessary features and capabilities into the refined design.
